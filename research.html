ICSSR Research Project
on Jal Jeevan Mission
Collobrator: Vikas Kumar (PI)
Project Title: The Jal Jeevan Mission and Its Role in Advancing Women Empowerment: An Evidence-Based Study in Diu, Union Territory
Fund Granted: 7,50,000 INR
This study examines the impact of a development program on villages, focusing on women's empowerment and institutional roles. It identifies challenges in implementation and suggests improvements. Using a mixed-methods approach, the research includes key-informant interviews, household surveys, stratified random sampling, and statistical analysis (descriptive statistics and chi-square test). The findings will help enhance program effectiveness and societal benefits.

HSI
Classification
Collobrator: Gaurav
In Hyperspectral Images (HSI) classification problem, each pixel in the HSI is classified into its underlying signature. Deep learning-based techniques outperform such supervised tasks given large labelled datasets. However, HSI ground truth labels are rarely available and difficult to annotate. Hence, I am exploring self-supervised learning, which performs well with limited labelled samples.

ML for
Solare Energy
Forecasting
Collobrator: Joanne Gomes, Ekta Desai
This work proposes a reduced yet highly relevant feature set for solar power prediction, combining four feature selection methods. It applies transfer learning across cities using XGBoost, Kernel Ridge, and MLP, significantly cutting computational costs and data requirements. The results demonstrate improved scalability and reliable forecasting performance, offering a novel approach to interpretable solar power prediction.

HSI
Unmixing
Collobrator: Prof. Manjunath V. Joshi, Jignesh Bhatt
Hyperspectral Images (HSI) have rich spectral details but low spatial resolution. I worked to find solutions using the regularization-based framework for spectral unmixing. Due to low spatial resolution, more than one material is mixed in a single pixel. The process of identifying and quantifying materials present in the pixel for the observed scene is called spectral unmixing.
